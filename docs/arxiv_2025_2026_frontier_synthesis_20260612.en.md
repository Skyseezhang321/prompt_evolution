# arXiv 2025/2026 Frontier Deep-Read Synthesis

Date: 2026-06-12

Scope: A **time-slice synthesis** of the 25 deep-read notes for 2025/2026 papers in the [literature map full index](literature_map.md), answering the question "What has been happening in this field over the past two years, and how does it differ from the era of classical methods?" Detailed per-paper evidence remains in the individual paper notes; the synthesis organized by reading batch is in [Batch 1–3 Synthesis](arxiv_deep_reading_batch3_synthesis.md). This document focuses solely on cross-paper timeline narrative and consensus/tension convergence — it does not replace any of those sources.

Scope and criteria: 25 papers = 10 from 2025 + 15 from 2026 under the literature-map year convention (GEPA arXiv first submission 2507, note filed under 2026; PROSE is a coin-flip internal baseline with no independent arXiv ID). arXiv first-submission IDs span 2502–2606; collection covers the top-80 search through 2026-06-08 plus subsequent targeted supplementary reads.

Evidence boundary: Paper-level synthesis. All material comes from existing deep-read notes; all numbers are cross-verified against the same note sources. **This is not a reproduction result from this project.** 2026 new submissions in particular should be independently reproduced before being treated as stable regularities.

## One-Page Conclusions

1. **The era of "name a new method" (2022–2024) is over.** Among the 25 papers in 2025/2026, only GEPA qualifies as "a named method that establishes a new main line." The rest produce diagnostics (is optimization worth it? did the edit actually help?), formalizations (multi-agent credit assignment), object upgrades (tool schema / pattern / codebook), self-evolution (the optimizer itself), and hygiene & regularization (anti-spurious-gain).
2. **A pre-optimization gate has become cross-paper consensus.** Coin Flip gives the strongest constraint (49% of 72 optimization runs on Haiku fall below zero-shot); APO-KG gives the positive condition (the more complex the schema, the clearer the gain; cross-dataset transfer leaves only ~1% F1 gain); Prompt Repetition gives the zero-cost comparison baseline (non-reasoning mode: 47/70 significant wins, 0 losses).
3. **"Score went up ≠ actually got better" now has an actionable detection surface.** PrefPO quantifies prompt hacking rate (TextGrad 86% vs PrefPO 37% vs Minimal 31%); TextReg turns overfitting into a regularization target (OOD multi-task +8~+11.8); Edit-Level causal analysis yields edit-family priors (meta_instruction × math = −0.103).
4. **The real theme of multi-agent optimization is credit assignment, not more agents.** MASPO (explicit misalignment modeling), MAPRO (MAP inference + belief propagation), and Temporal/Structural Credit (block updates by role/round) converge from three independent directions.
5. **The optimizer itself is now a managed, optimized artifact.** SePO ablation (removing self-improvement: 76.38→74.94), SPEAR (rigid loop significantly worse than free-form agent), and PrefPO (upgrading the optimizer +11.5 > upgrading the discriminator +6.1) support this from three directions.
6. **The real gap is reasoning models.** None of the 25 papers addresses bi-level / thought-driven (the o1/R1 era treats prompts as reasoning controllers); this is consistent with the conclusions of the [taxonomy completeness check](arxiv_taxonomy_completeness_check_20260610.md) and is the highest-priority registered direction for targeted search.

## Timeline: How Paper Form Has Shifted Over Two Years

Looking at arXiv first-submission months, 2025/2026 has a clear rhythm:

- **2025 H1 (2502–2507): the field takes stock of itself.** Two optimization-perspective surveys ([APO Survey](paper_notes/paper-apo-survey-2025.md), [APE Survey](paper_notes/paper-ape-survey-2025.md)) and the [Context Engineering Survey](paper_notes/paper-context-engineering-2025.md) successively frame the field within unified frameworks and call out the frontier; concurrently, [AutoPDL](paper_notes/paper-autopdl-2025.md) (2504) makes prompting patterns the primary search variable, [Scaling Textual Gradients](paper_notes/paper-scaling-textual-gradients-2025.md) (2506) exposes the context wall of textual gradients, and [APO-KG](paper_notes/paper-apo-kg-construction-2025.md) (2506) gives empirical evidence for "when optimization is worth doing."
- **July 2025: [GEPA](paper_notes/paper-gepa-2026.md) (2507) establishes a new main line.** Trajectory reflection + Pareto candidate retention; on Qwen3 8B, aggregate score 45.23→54.85, surpassing GRPO's 48.91, using on average only ~3,936 rollouts (GRPO fixed at 24,000). This is the only named method from 2025/2026 to enter the "seven-method main line."
- **2025 H2 (2508–2512): scaling up and cooling down in parallel.** [DistillPrompt](paper_notes/paper-distillprompt-2025.md) (2508) and [MAPRO](paper_notes/paper-mapro-2025.md) (2510) continue to broaden the optimization target; toward year-end two cooling shots appear — [Flawed Metaphor](paper_notes/paper-textual-gradients-flawed-metaphor-2025.md) (2512) dismantles the metaphorical shell of textual gradients, and [Prompt Repetition](paper_notes/paper-prompt-repetition-2025.md) (2512) shows that a zero-intelligence transform can match many optimization scenarios.
- **2026 H1 (2601–2606): 14 dense papers in half a year across three parallel lines.** Object upgrades ([JTPRO](paper_notes/paper-jtpro-2026.md), [MASPO](paper_notes/paper-maspo-2026.md), [Prompt Codebooks](paper_notes/paper-prompt-codebooks-2026.md), [Temporal/Structural Credit](paper_notes/paper-temporal-structural-credit-mas-2026.md), [Modular PO](paper_notes/paper-modular-prompt-optimization-2026.md)); self-evolution ([SePO](paper_notes/paper-sepo-2026.md), [MemAPO](paper_notes/paper-memapo-2026.md)); hygiene and diagnostics ([TextReg](paper_notes/paper-textreg-2026.md), [PrefPO](paper_notes/paper-prefpo-2026.md), [Edit-Level](paper_notes/paper-causal-edit-level-2026.md), [VISTA](paper_notes/paper-vista-reflection-dark-2026.md), [Coin Flip](paper_notes/paper-coin-flip-2026.md), [SPEAR](paper_notes/paper-spear-2026.md)). The most recent paper, SePO (2606), was submitted just days before this document's collection cutoff.

## Shift One: Ask Whether It's Worth It Before Asking Whether the Edit Was Right (Cooling Down & Diagnostics)

This is the most distinctive line of 2025/2026: the field has begun systematically interrogating itself.

- **[Coin Flip](paper_notes/paper-coin-flip-2026.md) (2604)** gives the strongest practical constraint: in a compound AI setup, 49% of Claude Haiku 4.5's 72 optimization runs fall below zero-shot; 14 of Nova Lite's 24 method×task means fall below zero-shot; agent-to-agent interaction shows F<1 and p>0.52 across all tests (very weak coupling — joint optimization lacks a prerequisite). Its actionable output is a pre-optimization gate: zero-shot baseline → 10–20 candidates to estimate headroom and noise floor → coupling test → stop if below threshold. The only task with positive returns across all methods is HelpSteer2 (best gain +6.8); best gains on other tasks are only +1.1 / +0.7 / +0.6.
- **PROSE (Appendix C of the same paper, [note](paper_notes/paper-prose-2026.md))** is the authors' own controlled counter-example: structured five-stage decomposition + six-operator evolution + risk-adjusted fitness (0.70·mean score + 0.15·Sharpe + 0.15·DRO), all enabled, yet no measurable robustness advantage — "adding risk engineering on the selection side" cannot rescue fragility. Note: it is an internal baseline, not an independent publication.
- **[VISTA](paper_notes/paper-vista-reflection-dark-2026.md) (2603)** gives reflection-based optimization a structural counter-example: on a defective GSM8K seed, GEPA drops from 23.81% to 13.50% (the root cause never enters its hypothesis space); decoupling "hypothesis generation about the root cause" from "rewriting based on the hypothesis" and running them in parallel recovers performance to 87.57%. On cross-model transfer, GEPA's optimized prompt yields only 22.74% on Qwen3-4B; VISTA still reaches 86.05%.
- **[Flawed Metaphor](paper_notes/paper-textual-gradients-flawed-metaphor-2025.md) (2512)** dismantles the metaphor with destructive ablations: wrong evaluation labels typically do not reduce test performance; 100-step training shows gradient-free overfitting; high-scoring prompts may rely on prevalence hacking (strongly forbidding the minority class "unknown"). The conclusion is that method comparisons must control for the number of candidates and the selection budget; otherwise credit for "gradients" may belong entirely to "more sampling."
- **[Prompt Repetition](paper_notes/paper-prompt-repetition-2025.md) (2512)** establishes the zero-cost comparison baseline: `<QUERY><QUERY>` in non-reasoning mode scores 47/70 significant wins and 0 losses across 7 models × 10 configurations (NameIndex: 21.33%→97.33%); padding controls have no effect; in reasoning mode only 5/28 groups win. Before any optimizer reports a gain, the first question should be whether it beats this zero-intelligence transform.
- **[APO-KG](paper_notes/paper-apo-kg-construction-2025.md) (2506)** is the positive-side piece of this picture: gains are clear for complex schemas / long inputs / strict formatting (ECR triple F1 up to +16%, rel=100: 0.62→0.72), but cross-dataset transfer leaves only ~1% F1 gain (in-dataset ~8% or more) — optimization is "a robustness tool under a complexity amplifier," not universal gain.

**Convergence:** "Is it worth optimizing?" now has an executable decision procedure (headroom / noise floor / coupling / zero-cost baseline) for the first time. "Did the edit actually help?" now has structural failure explanations (hypothesis space, prevalence hacking, transfer collapse) for the first time.

## Shift Two: Expanding the Optimization Target — From a Single Prompt to an Artifact Graph

Almost none of the methodology papers in 2025/2026 ask "how to edit one instruction segment" anymore; instead they disaggregate and enlarge the optimizable target:

- **[AutoPDL](paper_notes/paper-autopdl-2025.md)**: the pattern itself is the most important search variable — on FEVER, Granite 13B goes from 6.5% to 74.0% (the best configuration is 3-shot ReWOO, not a better instruction); but the optimal pattern varies strongly by task and model, and GPT-4o-mini shows no MBPP+ gain on transfer.
- **[JTPRO](paper_notes/paper-jtpro-2026.md)**: failures in tool-use agents are usually in per-tool schema and slot descriptions, not in global instructions — on ToolACE 1000 tools: o3-mini OSR 51.27→64.46, GPT-5 62.37→73.55; ETID GPT-4o mini 46.53→66.83.
- **[Modular PO](paper_notes/paper-modular-prompt-optimization-2026.md)**: fixed schema, section-local edits only — ARC-Challenge: LLaMA-3 8B 75.0→79.10 (TextGrad only 75.67); MMLU: 57.21→61.50 (TextGrad drops to 56.40). This is the most direct experimental design for "rollback-able optimization."
- **[Prompt Codebooks](paper_notes/paper-prompt-codebooks-2026.md)**: optimize prompts into routable, reusable instinct units keyed by input — HotpotQA: prompt length 14.1× shorter than MIPROv2, aggregate 9.6× shorter, while performance still exceeds GEPA (IFBench 41.33, +2.72).
- **[MASPO](paper_notes/paper-maspo-2026.md) / [MAPRO](paper_notes/paper-mapro-2025.md) / [Temporal-Structural Credit](paper_notes/paper-temporal-structural-credit-mas-2026.md)**: three multi-agent lines jointly formalize "who to update and which round" — MASPO uses misalignment cases + joint reward (Sequential MAS average 70.39 vs baseline 65.31; removing Joint Evaluate drops to 67.77); MAPRO uses max-product belief propagation for MAP inference (removing demonstration-guided reward: HumanEval-ET 80.21→76.04, CodeContest 31.52→29.70); Temporal/Structural Credit updates only low-credit roles or rounds (MedMCQA LLaMA3-8B Debate 55.13→64.63).
- **[SPEAR](paper_notes/paper-spear-2026.md)**: even the optimizer's tools and autonomy are variables — give the optimizer a Python sandbox for confusion-matrix / groupby-style error analysis; on industrial tasks: 0.760 vs TextGrad 0.110 (job location kappa), BBH-7 average 0.938 vs GEPA 0.628; stuffing the full DataFrame into context recovers only 2% of the gap; a rigid fixed loop is significantly worse than a free-form agent; 0 of 78 archived runs fall below seed (auto-rollback protects the lower bound).

**Convergence:** This project's v4 report summarizes this line as "the optimization target is now an artifact graph" (prompt, pattern, tool schema, slot, role, round, codebook, optimizer tools and strategy); versioning must also be upgraded to artifact-graph level. These 8 papers are the complete primary evidence for that judgment.

## Shift Three: From One-Shot Optimization to Self-Evolution and Experience Reuse

- **[GEPA](paper_notes/paper-gepa-2026.md)**: the main-line anchor. Trajectory reflection + Pareto frontier; Qwen3 8B aggregate 45.23→54.85 (GRPO 48.91, MIPROv2 47.84), ~3,936 rollouts vs GRPO's 24,000; ablation shows that Pareto selection itself contributes the most (GEPA +12.44 vs SelectBestCandidate +6.05); generated prompts are 9.2× shorter than MIPROv2. Boundaries are equally clear: on AIME-2025, 32.00 falls below GRPO's 38.00; degrades to ordinary rewriting when no reliable trace is available.
- **[SePO](paper_notes/paper-sepo-2026.md)**: brings the system prompt of "the prompt agent responsible for editing prompts" into the evolution loop — Manual-CoT 71.89→SePO-Generalist 76.38; removing self-improvement drops to 74.94; removing open-ended evolution drops to 72.64; multi-task pre-training cost $37.14 spread over five tasks is ~$7.43/task, and transfers to tasks not seen during pre-training (Sudoku 96.95→99.90). This is the core evidence for project insight 14, "optimizer/judge must also be versioned."
- **[MemAPO](paper_notes/paper-memapo-2026.md)**: success templates and error patterns stored in separate memories (CTM/EPM dual memory) — GPT-4o-mini average 70.7% vs TextGrad 63.6%, at 58.6% lower cost (average $0.31 vs $0.70); AQuA-RAT with dual memory: 61.7→82.5 (CTM-only / EPM-only reach only 80.5 / 77.9).
- **[DistillPrompt](paper_notes/paper-distillprompt-2025.md)**: examples are first distilled into task principles, then compressed and aggregated; average improvement across all datasets relative to Grips: +20.12% — another low-cost path for experience reuse.
- **[Scaling Textual Gradients](paper_notes/paper-scaling-textual-gradients-2025.md)**: full-batch textual gradients hit the context wall at ~50 examples; using historically high-scoring prompts as a momentum distribution for sampling (TSGD-M) — TREC 81.92→83.36, GSM8K 93.15→94.04 — "historical experience goes into the sampling distribution, not into the context."

**Convergence:** The engineering implication of self-evolution is not "let the system modify itself freely" but rather **bringing all artifacts on the optimizer side (its prompts, tools, memory, sampling distribution) under the same versioning, evaluation, and rollback discipline applied to task prompts**. SePO's ablation, SPEAR's rigid-loop comparison, and PrefPO's finding that "upgrading the optimizer (+11.5) is more effective than upgrading the discriminator (+6.1)" support the same conclusion from three independent directions.

## Shift Four: From "Score Went Up" to "Actually Got Better" (Hygiene, Regularization & Constraints)

- **[PrefPO](paper_notes/paper-prefpo-2026.md)**: hygiene is built into evaluation — BBH average 0.875 (MIPROv2 0.873, GEPA 0.870, TextGrad 0.850), while prompt length inflates only 4.7× (TextGrad 14.7×, Minimal variant 2.2×), repetition rate 0.044 (TextGrad 0.117, Minimal 0.012), prompt hacking rate 37% (TextGrad 86%, Minimal 31%, unoptimized baseline 12.8%). A prompt that is long, repetitive, and games the metric does not count as a successful optimization.
- **[TextReg](paper_notes/paper-textreg-2026.md)**: makes overfitting a regularization target — generates a regularization gradient alongside the task gradient to control length growth and rule narrowing; on OOD relative to TextGrad, multi-task +8~+11.8 (Phi-3.5-Mini Logical Deduction 5obj +11.8), while TextGrad/REVOLVE frequently fall below unoptimized CoT on OOD. Scope limitation is explicit: single-turn, rule-clear reasoning tasks only.
- **[Edit-Level causal analysis](paper_notes/paper-causal-edit-level-2026.md)**: observational analysis of 2,095 DSPy pairs + 17,708 TextGrad/GEPA pairs — meta_instruction × math = −0.103, clarity_constraint × logical = −0.083, extraneous_load × sequential = −0.060; 83% of association directions are stable under leave-one-out. Different tasks respond to the same edit family in opposite directions; "the clearer and more complex, the better" is a misconception.

**Convergence:** Combined with the prevalence hacking cases from Flawed Metaphor, these four papers together give a directly deployable "spurious-gain detection surface": length ratio, repetition ratio, seed similarity, edit family, minority-class performance, OOD/stress delta, hacking flag — these are the origin of this project's v4 engineering layer's release gate and ledger fields.

## Survey Layer: The Field's Self-Positioning

The three surveys (all 2025) produce no new methods but accomplish two things:

1. **Unified frameworks**: APO Survey uses a 5-stage process anatomy (seed → evaluation feedback → candidate generation → selection and retention → iteration depth); APE Survey uses three axes (optimization space × objective function (including constraints) × optimization method); Context Engineering Survey positions prompt optimization as a fundamental component under "Context Retrieval & Generation" in the larger context engineering picture — confirming that this project's scope of "black-box, discrete, natural-language prompt" is a reasonable narrow slice.
2. **Jointly naming the frontier**: constrained optimization (formalizing length/readability constraints — naturally adjacent to the hygiene empirics of Shift Four); multi-task and negative transfer; online/task-agnostic optimization; multi-objective Pareto. **Only APE Survey names bi-level / thought-driven (o1/R1 reasoning models as high-level controllers)** — and this is precisely the direction absent from all 25 empirical papers.

All three have been used in the [taxonomy external completeness check](arxiv_taxonomy_completeness_check_20260610.md): 7 clusters show no missing wholesale sections; what is missing is the above-mentioned frontier.

## Two Tensions: How Inconsistencies Converge into Applicability Conditions

The most valuable thing in 2025/2026 is not individual paper conclusions but the fact that "surface contradictions" between papers have converged into applicability conditions:

1. **Reflection is useful (GEPA) vs. reflection is harmful (VISTA).** Unified condition: whether the feedback signal contains trajectory-level diagnostic information and whether the true root cause falls within the optimizer's hypothesis space. Trace available, root cause visible → reflection-based optimization is extremely sample-efficient; root cause structurally absent → more reflection means more drift, requiring decoupling of hypothesis generation from rewriting.
2. **Large gains (JTPRO +13.19pp / AutoPDL +67.5pp / SPEAR 0.110→0.760) vs. coin flip (Coin Flip: 49% below zero-shot).** Unified condition: task structure. Complex schema, tool selection, strict formatting, latent capability not activated by default → optimization has exploitable structure. Free text, zero-shot already near ceiling, dev set small and noisy → optimization is expensive search through noise.

These two "tension → condition" convergences are something you can only obtain by reading the 2025/2026 literature as a whole. They are also the literature foundation for this project's six selection questions and pre-optimization gate.

## What 2025/2026 Has Not Yet Covered (Honest Inventory)

- **bi-level / thought-driven**: not a single paper among the 25; only APE Survey names it conceptually. The optimization of "prompt as reasoning controller" in the reasoning-model era is a wholesale-absent direction in the current library, registered as the highest-priority targeted search (top-80 sampling may simply not surface these — different search terms are needed).
- **Task-agnostic / online optimization**: inference-time optimization without a dev set in production, only tangentially touched by memory-style work.
- **Theoretical formulation for constrained optimization**: there is substantial empirical work on hygiene/length (Shift Four), but no representative paper with a formal solution in the style of "Γ(P)≤κ" has entered the library.
- **Multi-task negative transfer**: cross-task results from MemAPO/SePO are positive cases; systematic negative-transfer research is not covered.

## Inputs to This Project

1. **The audit chain is now complete.** Pre-optimization (Coin Flip gate + Prompt Repetition zero-cost baseline) → during optimization (trajectory/hypothesis/code three categories of feedback signal, artifact block editing) → post-optimization (PrefPO/TextReg/Edit-Level hygiene panel + OOD split) forms a complete closed loop; this is precisely the literature source for the v4 engineering layer's five-piece kit and release gate. No additional list is created here.
2. **The selection rationale for the first experiments is now stronger.** "Structured extraction / tool calling / strict formatting first, open-ended generation later" is now supported from both positive and negative sides by APO-KG, JTPRO, AutoPDL, and Coin Flip (see the minimum-experiment suggestions in [Batch 3 Synthesis](arxiv_deep_reading_batch3_synthesis.md)).
3. **Next collection actions are clear:** targeted search for bi-level / thought-driven; then supplement 1–2 representative papers for each of APE Survey's three axes: constrained / multi-task / online.
